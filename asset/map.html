FLOATING ISLAND

Suspended above an endless void, the Floating Island is where every second feels heavier than the last. Four players are dropped into a tight, unforgiving space where movement is everything and hesitation costs you more than just time. One of you holds the bomb, ticking louder with every step, forcing quick decisions and riskier plays as you chase, evade, and outmaneuver each other. The edges are never far, and a single misstep sends you falling into nothing. In this map, survival is not enough. You have to stay sharp, stay moving, and make sure the bomb is never yours when it reaches zero.

Sunny Clouds

Under clear skies and bright light, the Floating Island feels almost calm at first glance. Visibility is perfect, every movement is exposed, and there is nowhere to hide when the chase begins. With no environmental distractions, the pressure comes purely from the players. Clean movement, sharp timing, and quick decisions decide who keeps the bomb and who escapes it. It is a straight fight where skill speaks louder than anything else.

Sandstorm

A thick wave of dust rolls across the island, cutting visibility down to a few steps ahead. Players fade in and out of sight, turning every encounter into a sudden clash. Footsteps become harder to track, and the bomb carrier can disappear just long enough to turn the tables. In this chaos, awareness is limited and instincts take over.

Thunderclouds

Dark clouds gather overhead as thunder cracks through the air. Sudden flashes of lightning light up the island for a split second, revealing positions before plunging everything back into shadow. The constant shifts between light and dark make every move unpredictable. One moment you are hidden, the next you are fully exposed with the bomb ticking in your hands.

Blizzard

Freezing winds sweep across the Floating Island, covering it in a relentless whiteout. Vision is blurred and movement feels heavier as the storm pushes against you. Players struggle to keep track of each other while the bomb continues to count down. Every step feels uncertain, and every decision carries more weight when the world around you is barely visible.
